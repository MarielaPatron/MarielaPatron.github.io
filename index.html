Mariela Patron

Multimedia Journalist

I attend the University of California Berkeley Graduate School of Journalism.
 I like to code and use data graphics, video, audio and writing to report.
I cover politics, education, business, labor and citizen activism.
I worked at MSNBC after being chosen as a fellow for the NBC News Group Summer Fellows Program
in partnership with the National Association of Hispanic Journalists.
I graduated summa cum laude from the University of La Verne with a Bachelor of Arts in Journalism in 2015.
My coverage on adjunct professors' fight for unionization and students with disabilities won SPJ awards.
Through multimedia platforms, I want to expand news consumption in the English and Spanish markets.
I am a proud first generation Mexican American and Los Angeles native.
